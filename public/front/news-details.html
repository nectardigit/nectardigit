24 June 2021

Tata Motors introduces the new Winger Ambulance as per Ministry of Health & Population standards in Nepal

Speaking on the occasion, Mr. Rudrarup Maitra, Head – International Business, Commercial Vehicles, Tata Motors Ltd. said, “Tata Motors commercial vehicles have been well accepted by the people of Nepal and we will continue to introduce more models and variants in the country. With the Winger Ambulance, we are proud to have introduced the only Ambulance in Nepal, which complies with the AIS125 & MoHP’s standards in Nepal and comes equipped with all the necessary medical equipment. It is available across all the 3 categories of life support – C (Common-life support), B (Basic-life support) and A (Advanced life support). This ambulance will set new benchmarks with its car-like suspension, ideal for road conditions of Nepal, and modern features, making the Winger Ambulance the most preferred vehicle in its class. We’re confident that Sipradi Trading Pvt. Ltd. will support with sales and service for the Winger Ambulance with the same passion as they have been doing for other models from our line-up for many decades. We’re also confident that the Winger Ambulance will serve the country of Nepal and help in further improving the medical facilities of the country.”

Speaking about the new Tata Winger Ambulance, Mr. Rajan Babu Shrestha, CEO, Sipradi Trading Pvt. Ltd. said, “With the ongoing situation, every one of us are concerned about our health and wellbeing. In partnership with Tata Motors and as a socially responsible company, we have always shown our commitment to bring the products that will benefit the society as a whole. With the same spirit, we have brought the new Tata Winger Ambulance and we are confident that the Winger Ambulance will be ‘The Healing Vehicle for Nepal’. Tata Winger is a well-known vehicle and established product in Nepal, benefitting our customers from school, public transport, trades to the tourism industry. Now, with the launch of ambulance on the Winger platform, which complies with AIS 125 and Nepal Government Standard, we believe this will equally benefit the health sector of our country as well. With the support of Nepal Government, emergency vehicles such as ambulances can be purchased in duty free. We are glad to act as a medium to launch the smartly designed product for the good cause.”

  • Only ambulance in Nepal that complies with AIS 125 and MoHP standards
  • Available in all 3 categories for life support: Category-C (Common Life Support), Category-B (Basic Life Support) and Category-A (Advance Life Support)
  • Powered by the 2.2-litre DiCOR BSIV engine; with a flat torque ensuring unmatched performance and fuel efficiency
  • Category-C: comes equipped with twin AC and a stretcher-cum-bed with ample seating space for attendants.
  • Category-B: additionally offers ECG monitor and oxygen monitor, CPR board, catheterisation sets, splints and OB kit, manual suction set, nebuliser set and many more features.
